The guide

I'm Andreas. I walk — and I'd love you to walk with me.

I grew up in Hannover with a restless pair of boots. I walked the Camino Francés in 2023 — alone, across all of Spain — and something inside me settled. In November 2024 I moved to Tenerife. I walk every Sunday. And now I want to share these paths with people who feel the same pull.

Portrait — Andreas, candid on trail
How I got here

From Hannover to the Island of Fire

I was born in 1977 in Hannover, Germany. I speak German and Dutch as mother tongues, and English as a third language that became a daily one. For most of my life, walking was just something I did on weekends.

In 2023 I walked the Camino Francés for the first time — alone. Eight hundred kilometres from Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port to Santiago de Compostela. When I finished, I understood why people do it twice. In November 2024 I moved to Tenerife. The island caught me in a way I hadn't expected. The paths here are extraordinary. I want to show them to you.

The path so far
The two-generation walk

2023 alone. 2025 with my son Jason.

The first Camino was about finding out if I could do it. I could. One step at a time, across all of Spain, with only my thoughts and the path ahead.

The second time I walked it with my son Jason. We called it the two-generation walk — father and son, same path, the same blisters and the same awe. To hand something over that means everything to me, that is a feeling I cannot describe. You have to walk it to understand.
