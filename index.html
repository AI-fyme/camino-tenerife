Signature Experience

Camino Tenerife

Five days. One day at the volcano, two days in the mountains and two days on the Atlantic coast. On day one you walk the flanks of Teide with black lava and absolute silence, you only hear your own footsteps. Then the mountains: ridge paths above the clouds, ancient pine forests, and stunning views over coast. The final two days belong to the Atlantic with wild shores where the ocean still feels like it belongs to no one.

  • 5 Days / 4 Nights

    Fully guided from volcano to ocean, accommodation included.

  • Volcano · Mountains · Coast

    Three completely different landscapes across one island.

  • Small Groups Only

    Maximum 6 walkers, guided personally by Andreas.

The Philosophy

Why Walk With Me

Island Knowledge

I moved to Tenerife in 2024 and walk here every Sunday. I know where the path opens to a view no one expects, which trails the maps don't show, and where the forest goes quiet enough to hear only the wind.

Pure Pilgrim

I walked the Camino Francés twice. Not as a professional with a badge. As a pilgrim. When you walk the Camino, all titles fall away. You are just a person with boots and a path. That is the spirit I bring to every walk.

The Nature of Tenerife

I organise these walks for people who love nature. Not to show you a postcard. To let you feel what Tenerife actually is on foot: volcanic rock, ancient laurel forests, the Atlantic horizon. Nature the way you never experience it at home.

Tailored Pacing

Every walk is adapted to your rhythm. The point is not just to reach the destination, but to fully experience every step along the trail.

English. German. Dutch.

I guide fluently in all three languages. Whether you come from London, Amsterdam, or Munich, there is no language barrier on the trail. Just good conversation as you walk.

Meet your guide

One person, walking beside you.

I guide one small group at a time. No crowds, no rush. I walked the Camino Francés twice and moved to Tenerife because of what this island does to you when you slow down and go on foot.
